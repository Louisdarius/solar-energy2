Energy the source of our life

Environmental issues of today are mainly caused by our production and the amount of carbon dioxide we emit everyday (consumption of energy)- whether that is driving cars, manufacturing goods or simply boiling a kettle. Our energy use impacts the earth’s natural systems in ways we may not be aware of, so it is essential that we become energy efficient and switch to low-carbon fuels in order to lower emission. My project is about solving environmental issues caused by non-renewable resources of energy, by using a sustainable energy source; such as solar panel. However, it is important to understand the important of energy in our life and its consuming costs.

Energy is essential to life and all living organisms. It is central to human activity for preparing food, warming homes, powering travel, and producing goods. It also enhances and support health in many ways. One of the most important sources of energy is the sun. The energy of the sun is the original source of most of the energy found on earth. We get solar heat energy from the sun, and sunlight can also be used to produce electricity from solar cells.

Growing population

Global demand for energy is rapidly increasing, due to population and economic growth, especially in large emerging countries. Availability of energy allows populations to grow. Growing populations consume more energy, the more energy consumed, the more carbon dioxide we emit into the air and the bigger the impact on the environment.

The Cost of Energy

As the population grows so is the energy costs. The energy being indispensable, its production and consuming costs should be easily accessible and affordable but that’s not the case. Energy suppliers compete on price and service to win customers. In setting their prices, suppliers will seek to cover their costs and to make a profit. There are many factors that causes the increase of energy prices such as:

Market forces

The wholesale price of gas par example to go up, which then means energy customers/consumers are charged a higher bill.

Limited supply

Limitation in supply is also another factor. Gas is pumped out of the ground and electricity is mostly generated using a mix of fossil fuels like oil, gas and coal. These are all-natural supplies, which are costly to get hold of, and more importantly, in limited supply.

Global conflicts

Wars in oil-rich countries and conflicts between countries over gas pipelines can impact wholesale prices. For example, wholesale gas prices in the UK have spiked when supplies in Ukraine, Iraq and Syria have been threatened in recent years.

Solar Panel, The Solution To Environmental Issues

An increasing number of households are choosing to install solar panels on their home to help reduce their energy costs. By using the power that you’ve generated on your own roof, you can save hundreds each year and become less reliant on the power grid. More widely, solar power is also a great sustainable, green energy choice. Solar energy doesn’t only help reduce your electricity bills; it also has huge benefits for our planet such as: using less water, reducing air pollution, help to slow climate change, reducing your household’s carbon footprint and Reducing our reliance on fossil fuels.
